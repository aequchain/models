#### Section 8: Sustainability Verification - 7 Pillars

**Pillar 1: Material Sustainability**

- **Requirement**: 100% renewable/recyclable, non-toxic materials
- **Implementation**:
  - Wood: FSC-certified, reclaimed, or from E2 bamboo
  - Metal: Recycled content from E2, E8 scrap recovery
  - Plastics: Bio-based from E2, recycled filament programs
  - Textiles: Organic cotton, hemp, recycled fibers from E2
  - Electronics: Conflict-free minerals, e-waste recycling to E2
  - Consumables: Non-toxic, biodegradable where possible
- **Verification**: Material sourcing audit, supplier certifications, lifecycle analysis
- **Status Target**: 100% by Year 3

**Pillar 2: Energy Sustainability**

- **Requirement**: 100% renewable energy (production + operation)
- **Implementation**:
  - Solar PV arrays on facility roofs (50-200 kW depending on scale)
  - Wind turbines where feasible (micro-scale: 5-20 kW)
  - Battery storage for load balancing (50-200 kWh)
  - Energy-efficient equipment (LED lighting, high-efficiency motors)
  - Smart energy management systems
  - Grid connection for surplus energy sale
- **Verification**: Energy audit, renewable energy certificates, consumption tracking
- **Status Target**: 100% by Year 2

**Pillar 3: Waste Sustainability**

- **Requirement**: Zero waste, full circularity
- **Implementation**:
  - Wood waste → E5 for biochar or E2 for mycelium substrates
  - Metal scrap → E2, E8 for recycling and remanufacturing
  - Plastic waste → E2 for recycling into filament
  - Textile waste → E2 for mycelium substrates or fiber recovery
  - E-waste → E2 for component recovery and material recycling
  - Organic waste → E1, E5 for composting or carbonization
  - Packaging → Reusable containers, compostable materials
- **Verification**: Waste audit, diversion rate tracking, material flow analysis
- **Status Target**: 99% diversion by Year 3

**Pillar 4: Water Sustainability**

- **Requirement**: Minimal use, no contamination, cycling
- **Implementation**:
  - Rainwater harvesting for non-potable uses (5,000-20,000 L storage)
  - Greywater recycling for equipment cooling, cleaning
  - Diamond electrode water treatment (from E11) for purification
  - Low-flow fixtures and water-efficient equipment
  - Closed-loop cooling systems for lasers, CNC machines
  - Monitoring and leak detection systems
- **Verification**: Water audit, consumption tracking, quality testing
- **Status Target**: 80% reduction vs. conventional by Year 3

**Pillar 5: Social Sustainability**

- **Requirement**: Fair labor, community benefit, accessible
- **Implementation**:
  - Equidistributed salaries (aequchain smart contracts)
  - Sliding scale memberships based on income
  - Free access programs for underserved communities
  - Youth education and apprenticeship programs
  - Inclusive design (wheelchair accessibility, adaptive tools)
  - Diverse hiring and leadership practices
  - Mental health and well-being support
- **Verification**: Social effect assessment, member demographics, satisfaction surveys
- **Status Target**: 100% compliance from Day 1

**Pillar 6: Economic Sustainability**

- **Requirement**: Tends toward free, equidistributed

  - Aequchain profit distribution (see Section 9)

```solidity
// E7_OpenStudio Smart Contract (Conceptual)

contract E7_OpenStudio {
    // State Variables
    mapping(address => Member) public members;
    mapping(address => Employee) public employees;
    mapping(uint256 => Project) public projects;
    mapping(uint256 => Equipment) public equipment;
    
    uint256 public totalRevenue;
    uint256 public operatingCosts;
    uint256 public treasuryBalance;
    uint256 public contributionRate; // % to network treasury
    
    struct Member {
        address wallet;
        uint256 membershipTier; // 1=Basic, 2=Pro, 3=Business
        uint256 joinDate;
        uint256 hoursUsed;
        uint256 projectsCompleted;
        uint256 skillCertifications;
        uint256 contributionScore;
        bool active;
    }
    
    struct Employee {
        address wallet;
        uint256 role; // 1=Technician, 2=Instructor, 3=Manager
        uint256 hireDate;
        uint256 contributionScore;
        uint256 valueGenerated;
    }
    
    struct Project {
        uint256 id;
        address creator;
        string description;
        uint256 equipmentUsed;
        uint256 materialsConsumed;
        uint256 hoursSpent;
        bool completed;
        bool openSource; // If true, design shared to commons
    }
```
